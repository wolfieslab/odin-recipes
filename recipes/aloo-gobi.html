Aloo Gobi

Description:

Aloo Gobi is a classic North Indian vegetarian dish made with tender potatoes (aloo) and cauliflower (gobi), cooked together in a fragrant blend of spices. This dry curry is known for its warm, earthy flavors, subtle heat, and golden hue, thanks to turmeric. Infused with cumin, garlic, ginger, and garam masala, Aloo Gobi is a comforting, wholesome dish that pairs beautifully with roti, paratha, or steamed rice. It's simple to prepare, making it a staple in Indian households and a favorite for weeknight dinners or festive meals alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium potatoes – peeled and diced
  • 1 small cauliflower – cut into small florets
  • 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable or mustard oil)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 medium onion – finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes – finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt – to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves) – chopped, for garnish

Steps

  1. Vegetables (Optional for quicker cooking):Boil potatoes and cauliflower until just tender (not mushy). Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat Oil & Temper Spices:Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Sauté Aromatics:Add onion and cook until golden.Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies; sauté for 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add Tomatoes & Spices:Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and oil separates. Add turmeric, coriander, cumin powder, salt, and red chili powder (if using).
  5. Cook the Veggies:Add potatoes and cauliflower to the masala. Mix well to coat with spices. Cover and cook on low heat for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is tender.
  6. Finish with Garam Masala:Sprinkle garam masala and mix gently.
  7. Garnish & Serve:Top with chopped cilantro. Serve hot with roti, paratha, or rice.
